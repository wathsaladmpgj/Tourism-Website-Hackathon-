About Sinhala and Tamil New Year

The Sinhala and Tamil New Year, celebrated in April, is a major cultural event in Sri Lanka. It signifies the traditional solar New Year and is marked by unique rituals, delicious food, and family gatherings. Traditional games, cooking rituals, and blessings for a prosperous year are key highlights of this vibrant festival.

About Vesak Poya

Vesak Poya, also known as Buddha Purnima or Wesak, is the most sacred Buddhist festival that commemorates the birth, enlightenment (Bodhi), and death (Parinirvana) of Gautama Buddha. It is celebrated during the full moon in May. It is a time for reflection on the teachings of Buddha, and devotees engage in meditation, offer prayers, and participate in acts of kindness. Temples are decorated with lights, and special ceremonies are held. Devotees often light lanterns, release birds or animals as a symbolic act of compassion, and share food with the less fortunate.

About Esala Perahara

Esala Perahera is a grand cultural and religious festival held in Kandy, Sri Lanka, typically in July or August. It honors the Sacred Tooth Relic of the Buddha, housed in the Temple of the Tooth (Sri Dalada Maligawa). The festival is a mix of Buddhist and Hindu traditions, reflecting the cultural heritage of Sri Lanka. It seeks blessings for good rains and prosperity. The highlight is the magnificent procession featuring caparisoned elephants, traditional drummers, dancers, fire performers, and the sacred relic carried in a golden casket. It spans several days, culminating in a spectacular grand procession.

About Poson Poya

Poson Poya is a significant Buddhist festival celebrated in Sri Lanka in June. It commemorates the introduction of Buddhism to Sri Lanka by Arahat Mahinda, the son of Emperor Ashoka of India, in the 3rd century BCE. It marks the moment Sri Lanka embraced Buddhism as a way of life, which greatly influenced its culture and traditions. Devotees visit temples for prayers and rituals. Mihintale, believed to be the site of Arahat Mahinda's arrival, becomes a focal point for pilgrims. Acts of generosity, such as offering free meals (Dansals), are common.

About Thaipongal

Thaipongal, or Pongal, is a harvest festival celebrated by Tamil communities, typically around January 14th. It is dedicated to the Sun God (Surya) for blessing the harvest and providing sustenance. It marks the start of the Tamil month Thai and symbolizes prosperity and thanksgiving for nature's bounties. Families prepare the traditional dish "Pongal" made from newly harvested rice, jaggery, and milk. Homes are adorned with kolams (rangoli), and offerings are made to the Sun God. Cows are honored as symbols of agriculture and livelihood.

About Deepawali

Deepawali, or Diwali, is the Hindu festival of lights, celebrated in October or November. It signifies the triumph of light over darkness and good over evil. It commemorates various events in Hindu mythology, such as Lord Rama's return to Ayodhya after defeating Ravana and the victory of Lord Krishna over the demon Narakasura. Homes are cleaned and decorated with oil lamps (diyas) and rangoli. People wear new clothes, exchange gifts, and enjoy festive sweets. Fireworks are lit, and prayers are offered to Goddess Lakshmi for wealth and prosperity.
